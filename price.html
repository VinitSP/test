Market Trends in Chatbot Pricing

You have two options for getting a chatbot: either create one specifically for your business or use one that's already made.

1. In-House Chatbot Development Costs:

Now engineering an ai chatbot is not a task of one person that’s why this task demands seven to ten people to build a chatbot, including a project manager, a chatbot designer, a developer proficient in Python, JavaScript, and Node.js, a data scientist, and someone to oversee everything and ensure a smooth launch. The more people you hire for this purpose, the easier it will be to build a chatbot that meets the needs of your business.

Read More

But not every business can afford such a dedicated team. So let's consider the key roles needed for in-house chatbot development:

  • Chatbot Designer: Salary ranges from $38,000 to $91,000 per year, with a median salary of $58,792/year.
  • Chatbot Developer: Salary ranges from $45,000 to $112,000 per year, with a median salary of $70,554/year.
  • Data Scientist: Salary ranges from $78,000 to $205,000 per year, with a median salary of $126,040/year.

These salary estimates are based on the US labor market. By looking at the above salary as a business owner you can estimate how much money you need to pay to all these people to create a chatbot so obviously getting a chatbot developed from scratch is not something for small and medium-sized businesses.

2. Costs of Hiring a Chatbot Agency:

When you see how much you'd pay in salaries, you might think about hiring a chatbot agency instead. Usually, it's cheaper because outsourcing is supposed to save you money. These agencies can make chatbots tailored to your business without you having to hire your own team.

However, there are some disadvantages to outsourcing as well. You won't have enough command of how things are done, and you might notice differences in quality and commitment.

On average, chatbot agencies charge between $10,000 and $50,000 for a simple project, but if your project is more complicated, it could cost even more.

3. Using chatbot as a Service Pricing:

While creating a custom chatbot sounds impressive, most businesses don't need such a complex solution for customer service or sales automation. Unless you're a major brand with vast resources, you can likely meet your needs with chatbot tools.

These tools are much cheaper, starting from $0, and offer various features, integrations, and customization options.

One such chatbot company that offers such low-cost chatbots is Robofy, our offer starts at $0 for chatbot free trials and goes up to 349$ per month and $3499 yearly which encompasses tons of features, and a large of integration options.

Factors Influencing Chatbot Pricing

Complexity and Features: As the chatbot gets more complex, with more topics it can talk about, a better understanding of language, and extra features like identifying feelings or speaking different languages, the cost goes up.
System Integrations: Connecting the chatbot to existing business systems like customer databases or sales software is important for it to work well, but it can make the price higher, especially if it needs extra technical procedure work to connect them.
Channel Integrations: How much does a chatbot cost also depends on how many places the chatbot will be, like websites, social media, or messaging apps. Each place might need different ways to connect the chatbot.
Data Preparation and Training: To make a chatbot smart, it needs to learn from data. This means gathering data, getting it ready, and teaching the chatbot using that data, which all costs money.
Continuous Learning: The chatbot has to keep learning to stay good at its job and handle new questions. This learning needs to happen regularly, which means spending money regularly.
Scalability: Making sure the chatbot can handle more users or do more things later can change how much a chatbot costs at the beginning and later on. Spending more money at first to plan for this can actually save money later.
Marketing and Promotion: Nowadays, the more a thing is shown to people, the higher its demand goes, and the more people know about it. So, it requires spending money to tell people about chatbots and get them to use it is also something to think about when planning the budget, which ultimately affects its cost.
Updates and Upgrades: Just like your phone or computer needs updates, the chatbot needs them too to stay useful, which is only possible with time-to-time upgradation. This means spending money on making it better and keeping it up to date.
Support and Troubleshooting: When things go wrong, or the chatbot needs help, someone has to fix it. This could be the people who made the chatbot or someone in your company, which adds to the cost of running it.

However, the above-mentioned criteria are not the only ones, there are other factors exist too that influence the chatbot pricing including whether it’s a customized chatbot or platform-based solution, or If the chatbot uses third-party APIs or services for additional capabilities, plus if the chatbot complies with regulations such as GDPR, HIPAA, or others relevant to the industry or geography.
